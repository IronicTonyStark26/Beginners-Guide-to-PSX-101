1. Accounts Opening & Stakeholders

Before investing, you need the following accounts and stakeholders as per Mashal Khan's guidance:

1.1 Bank Account (Islamic)

Open a Sharia-compliant account with Meezan Bank (recommended), Faysal Bank Islamic, or Bank Alfalah Islamic.

  • Ensure account is linked to your CNIC
  • Enable online banking for easy fund transfers

1.2 Mutual Fund Account

Invest through an AMC (Asset Management Company) such as:

  • Meezan Fund Managers
  • Faysal Asset Management
  • Al Meezan Funds

Choose Sharia-compliant funds (equity, balanced, or income funds) and optionally start a SIP (Systematic Investment Plan).

1.3 CDC & VPS (Brokerage Account)

To invest in PSX directly, open a Central Depository Company (CDC) + VPS account via a bank-linked broker:

  • Arif Habib, AKD, KTrade (Sharia-compliant screening available)
  • Link your bank account for deposits/withdrawals

2. First Investment Steps

As a beginner, Mashal Khan recommends starting small and building confidence:

  • Begin with Sharia-compliant mutual funds rather than stocks to reduce risk.
  • Start a monthly SIP with PKR 5,000 – 10,000 depending on your budget.
  • Once comfortable, gradually add Sharia-compliant PSX stocks via your VPS account.
  • Focus on diversified investments to spread risk.

Use her recommended order:

  1. Mutual funds first
  2. Small PSX stock investments next
  3. Track performance and adjust monthly contributions

3. Long-term vs Short-term Investments

Understanding investment horizons is key:

3.1 Short-term

  • Frequent trades in PSX
  • Higher risk and potential volatility
  • Only for money you can afford to keep liquid

3.2 Long-term

  • Invest in mutual funds or solid Sharia-compliant stocks for 3-10 years
  • Use SIPs to build wealth gradually
  • Compounding effect maximizes returns over time

Mashal Khan’s advice: Beginners should focus on long-term investing first, short-term trading only after gaining experience.

4. Guidelines & Risk Management

  • Diversify across mutual funds and stocks to reduce risk
  • Set a monthly investment goal and stick to it
  • Start small, increase gradually as you learn
  • Keep all investments Sharia-compliant if following Islamic guidelines
  • Track market news and Mashal Khan’s guidance videos for ongoing education
